Drought Management

Droughts have become increasingly frequent due to climate change, affecting small-scale farmers and rural communities. Prolonged periods without sufficient rainfall lead to crop failures, reduced livestock productivity, and water scarcity, threatening both food security and household income.

Impacts

  • Reduced crop yields and failed harvests
  • Decreased livestock health and productivity
  • Soil degradation and loss of fertility
  • Increased food prices and economic hardship
  • Heightened community vulnerability and migration pressures

Mitigation Strategies

  • Planting drought-resistant crops and varieties
  • Mulching to retain soil moisture and reduce evaporation
  • Implementing rainwater harvesting systems and water storage
  • Promoting efficient irrigation techniques (drip irrigation, furrow irrigation)
  • Community awareness programs and early warning systems for drought preparedness

Long-Term Solutions

Long-term drought management requires integrated approaches, including reforestation, soil regeneration, agroforestry, and collaboration between government, NGOs, and local communities. Building resilience ensures that rural gardens remain productive despite changing rainfall patterns.

Greenhouse Gardening

Greenhouse gardening allows controlled growth environments for plants, extending seasons and improving yields.

Types of Greenhouses

  • Cold Frame
  • Hoop House
  • Glass Greenhouse
  • Polycarbonate Structure

Benefits

Protection from pests, better climate control, and year-round cultivation.
